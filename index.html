Constantinos Papacharalambous

UCL Mechanical Engineering, UC Santa Barbara (year abroad)

I chose engineering because I believe the most fulfilling work comes from solving meaningful problems, ones you genuinely care about. I am not driven by a fixed career title. My ambition is guided by a simple compass: to wake up every day ready to do what I love. Engineering gives me the analytical tools to break down complex challenges, but what excites me is applying them at the intersection of things I care about, whether that's health, sport, or technology.

I am a Mechanical Engineering master's student at University College London, where I am currently leading a year-long engineering project in collaboration with Moorfields Eye Hospital. We are developing a pressure-sensing injection needle for real-time intraocular pressure monitoring during the treatment of severe hypotony, a condition where the eye partially loses its structural volume. The goal is to enable clinicians to restore eye pressure safely in a single session, rather than over weeks of repeat visits.

Outside of academics, I am training for triathlon, currently ranked #1 in Cyprus for my age group in Ironman 70.3 competition. I love ski racing and surfing whenever I get the chance.

I am drawn to building, particularly in environments with high ownership, fast iteration, and close collaboration with ambitious people. A year spent in California solidified this, immersing me in a culture where experimentation and learning from failure were actively encouraged.

I also served 14 months in the Cyprus National Guard Special Forces, ranked 2nd and promoted to platoon leader. The most valuable lesson I took away was to value my time and to act on what I really want.
